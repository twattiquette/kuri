difficulty:

You're a trainee at a spy academy, learning to hold a cover under pressure. Most missions drop you in as the sole decider: read your cover, then choose the action they'd actually take. Stay in character and the moment passes clean; break character and you give off a tell, and every tell chips away at your nine lives.

Other missions flip the lens into a whodunnit: you're shown a profile one suspect claims as their alibi, then observe each suspect across three scenes. The innocent mostly hold true, bar the odd slip; the imposter, faking a disposition that isn't theirs, slips scene after scene. Read the room and accuse the cat whose tells form a pattern. Misread it and the tell lands on you.

Spend all nine lives and your cover's blown, the run's over. Maintain your cover, read the room, watch for tells, and climb from Recruit to Master Spy.

kuri (pronounced "qree", /ˈkjʊri/) is fiction, built as a game. All scenarios invented for entertainment by someone with zero clearance for anything. Resemblance to real people or organisations? Failure of imagination. Nothing here is advice, and treating a spy game as tradecraft is a you problem. No warranties; play at own risk.
